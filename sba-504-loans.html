504 Loan Structure

Two components: conventional bank loan (50%) and SBA-backed CDC debenture (40%). Borrower provides 10% equity. In bankruptcy, you may have two creditors with different lien positions.

Real Estate Collateral

Bank holds first lien, CDC holds second. Liens survive discharge. To keep the property, continue paying or include in a plan. If you surrender, the deficiency is dischargeable.

Strategies

Surrender and discharge deficiency in Ch.7, restructure through Subchapter V, negotiate a short sale, or deed in lieu of foreclosure.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I keep my building?

Yes, through Chapter 13 or Subchapter V. Continue regular payments and cure arrears through the plan.

What if the property is underwater?

Surrender and discharge the deficiency in Ch.7. Or in Subchapter V, modify the loans.

Is the CDC loan treated differently?

Both are secured claims. The bank has priority. If property value does not cover both, the CDC's unsecured deficiency is general unsecured.
