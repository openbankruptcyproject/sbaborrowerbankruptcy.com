SBA Borrower Bankruptcy

Discharge SBA Loans and Personal Guarantees

SBA Loans Are Dischargeable

Despite what many business owners believe, SBA loans -- including 7(a), 504, EIDL, and microloans -- are fully dischargeable in bankruptcy. There is no special exception for government-backed business loans. Your personal guarantee liability is eliminated upon discharge, and the SBA cannot pursue you personally afterward.

The key question is not whether the debt can be discharged, but which chapter of bankruptcy best fits your situation: Chapter 7 for a clean break, Chapter 13 for individual repayment, or Subchapter V Chapter 11 for business reorganization.

The Personal Guarantee Problem

SBA loans require personal guarantees from business owners with 20% or more ownership. When the business fails, the personal guarantee makes you individually liable for the full loan balance -- potentially hundreds of thousands of dollars. The SBA can pursue your personal assets, garnish wages, intercept tax refunds, and file lawsuits.

Bankruptcy eliminates this personal guarantee obligation. In Chapter 7, it is discharged completely in about 4 months. In Chapter 13 or Subchapter V, you pay what you can afford over 3-5 years. The discharge injunction permanently bars the SBA from collecting on your guarantee.

Your Options by SBA Loan Type

SBA 7(a) Loans: The most common SBA loan. Personal guarantee is dischargeable. Collateral liens survive but personal liability is eliminated.

SBA 504 Loans: Real estate and equipment loans with two lien holders (bank + CDC). Can surrender property and discharge the deficiency, or restructure through Subchapter V.

EIDL Loans: COVID-era disaster loans. Fully dischargeable with no special restrictions. See our companion site for detailed EIDL bankruptcy guidance.

SBA Microloans: Loans up to $50,000 through nonprofit intermediaries. May not require personal guarantees. Fully dischargeable.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can SBA loans be discharged in bankruptcy?

Yes. SBA loans -- including 7(a), 504, EIDL, and microloans -- are fully dischargeable. Unlike student loans, there is no special exception. Personal guarantee liability is eliminated upon discharge.

What happens to my personal guarantee in bankruptcy?

Your personal guarantee obligation is discharged. In Chapter 7, immediately. In Chapter 13 or Subchapter V, you pay what you can afford over 3-5 years and the remainder is discharged. The SBA cannot pursue you personally after discharge.

Can I keep my business and file bankruptcy?

Yes. Subchapter V of Chapter 11 allows you to restructure SBA debt while continuing operations. You remain in control. Most small businesses with SBA debt qualify for Subchapter V if total debts are under $7.5 million.
